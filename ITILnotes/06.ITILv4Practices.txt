1. Information Security Management 
	- protecting an organization by understanding and managing risks to the confidentialitym integrity and availability of information
	- protecting from bad actors
	- protecting assest
	- protection from hackers
	- making sure people who need access, have the access 

2. Relationship Management 
	- establishing and nurturing links between an organization and its stakeholders at strategic and tactical levels
	- relationships with all stakeholders 
	- the community 

3. Supplier Management 
	- ensure an organization's suppliers and their performance are managed appropriately to support provision of seamless, quality products and services 

4. Availability Management 
	- ensuring services deliver agreed levels of availability to meet customer and user needs
	- keep things up and running, and if they go down - get them up quick

5. Capacity and Performance Management 
	- ensuring services achieve agreed and expectred performance, satisfying and future demand in a cost-effective way

6. IT Asset Management 
	- Planning and managing the full lifecycle of all IT assets
	- IT Asset is every valuable component that can contribute to the delivery of an IT product or Service. 

7.Service Continuity Management 
	- ensuring service availability and performance is maintanined at a sufficient level in the event of a disaster

8. Monitoring and Event Management 
	- Systematically observing services and service components, and recording and reporting selected changes of state identidied as events
	- telemetry - detect things worth managing 
	- getting information from the systems, so you can keep them up and running 

9. Release Management 
	- Depolyment process
	- Making new and changed services and features available for use 
	- "zoom" level of release

10. Service Configuration Management 
	- ensuring accurate and reliable information about the configuration of services and the configuration items that support them is available when and where needed
	- when you make decissions, shen you're planning that you have good enough detailed info abou the configuration of your service and products 
	- get right to the information
 
11. Deployment Management 
	- moving new or changed hardware, software, documentation, processesm or any other service componet to live enviroments
	- honors the DevOps practise of continuous deployment, continous integration, continuous testing to move faster to value in smaller chunks and get features to consumers 

12. Continual Improvement 
	- aligning an organization's practices and services with changing needs through ongoing identification and improvement of all elemts of effective management of products and services

13. Change Control 
	- ensuring risks are properly assessed, authorizing changes to proceed, and managing a change schedule to maximize the number of successful IT changes 
	- knowing what changed, minimizing the business disruption 
	- change practice to better suit Cloud mobile enviroments, and DevOps, Agile and Lean practices

14. Incident Management 
	-  practice that goes after things that are broken, to get them fixed quickly, and where things that are broken can range from a service being down to an individual user having trouble using new features of their software. 

15. Problem Management 
	- reducing the likelihood and impact of incidents by identifying actual and potential causes of incidents, and managing workarounds and known errors. 
	- go after the root cause......... why are they occuring... well, the why is that underlying problem 

16. Service Request Management 
	- Supporting the agreed quality of a service by handiling all prefedined, user-initiated service requests in an effective and user-friendly way 
	- users that are entitled to the product, fixing their needs, based on their subscription to your service or product. 

17. Service Desk 
	- capturing demand for incident resolution and service requests  
	- practice for capturing demand for incident resolution and service requests and also acting as a communication concentration point between the provider and its users 

18. Service Level Management 
	- setting clear business-based targets for service performance so that the delivery of a service can be properly assessed, monitored, and managed against these targets
